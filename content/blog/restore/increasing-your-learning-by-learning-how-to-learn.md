During Christmas holidays I took a course called [Learning How to Learn](https://href.li/?https://www.coursera.org/learn/learning-how-to-learn). Learning How to Learn is taught by Dr. Barbara Oakley along with other contributors. Now I sign up for online courses every now and then but I seldom complete them. If there is a statistic out there which tells us about a percentage of people who sign up for courses but never complete, then I’m sure to be on it. This course was different. I started the course with a bias. The bias was my personal agenda to improve my learning. Remember that not all biases are bad. Right from the first video, I was hooked. And the structure of the course kept my interest levels high. The structure:

1.  What is Learning?
2.  Chunking
3.  Procrastination and Memory
4.  Renaissance Learning and Unlocking Your Potential

Dr. Barbara Oakley introduces the topics in short videos which are easy to grasp. I would go through a module in the morning and then think about it during the day. This too I picked up from the course. It does not matter what your field is, or where you are in the stage of life (student, professional, retired etc.), this course will be worth your time and effort. The price is FREE. The value will last with you forever.
