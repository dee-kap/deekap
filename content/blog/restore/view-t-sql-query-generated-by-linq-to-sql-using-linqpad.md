In my [previous post](http://www.deepakkapoor.net/2008/09/view-t-sql-query-generated-by-linq-to.html) I showed you three different ways to capture the T-SQL query generated by LINQ To SQL. A comment on the post reminded me that I forgot to mention a fourth way i.e. using [Linqpad](http://www.linqpad.net/). In Linqpad when you have executed the query you can click on the SQL tab next to Results and look at the T-SQL query.

![image](https://googledrive.com/host/0B6PDO8HPEQZNZWpTRms0ZWtlaUU/uploads/2008/09/image11.png)

Other than viewing generated queries, Linqpad is also a great tool for writing queries. The tool itself is very lightweight and comes with many templates to get you started.
