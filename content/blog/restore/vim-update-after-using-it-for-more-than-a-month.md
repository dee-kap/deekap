It has now been close to a month since I [started using Vim](http://sydlog.io/2014/04/starting-vim-journey-again/) as my main editor. This post is about my Vim adventures.

Lately I have found watching videos to be an excellent source of learning new things. To increase my knowledge of Vim, I started by watching this excellent video titled [Write code faster: expert-level vim](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SkdrYWhh-8s) by Ben Orenstein. Other than many cool tricks Ben shows in this video, the point which really hit home for me was to **learn features in small proportions**. For a complex editor such as Vim, I think this is an invaluable suggestion. I followed it and I can report positive results.

Next, I turned to the famous tutorials by [Derek Wyatt](http://derekwyatt.org/). Following Ben Orenstien's suggestion, I watched only two videos by Derek Wyatt:

2.  [Basic Movement (Screencast 1)](http://vimeo.com/6170479)

3.  [Basic Movement (Screencast 2)](http://vimeo.com/6185584)

The benefit I got out of watching these videos was that I found myself comfortable using Vim, enjoying it, and most importantly increasing my curiosity levels to learn more.

Many people have talked about the initial days or weeks of frustration. For me that period of frustration lasted few hours. Once I had a grip on basic movements, I was comfortable doing simple tasks.

I will keep posting my progress on Vim and anything interesting I find in Vim-land.
